About Us


The Career Horizons Project, funded by the U.S. Department of Education Rehabilitation Services Administration (RSA), improves Competitive Integrated Employment (CIE) services for Mississippi residents between the ages of 16 and 70 who have a disability accompanied by or resulting from Long COVID syndrome.

Mississippi State University was one of 27 grantees across the nation chosen out of more than 200 applicants to receive the award from the Disability Innovation Fund program. This five-year grant project will run from October 1, 2024, to September 30, 2029.

Project Goals

  • Develop targeted outreach and enrollment strategies that identify youth and adults in MS (ages 16-70) who have acquired disabilities accompanied by or resulting from Long COVID since 2020.
  • Establish seamless statewide partnerships including employer networks, a Project Advisory Council (PAC), Mississippi Department of Rehabilitation Services (MDRS), and numerous other agencies that promote competitive integrated employment (CIE) for members of the target population.
  • Design and implement an innovative transition and career re-integration intervention with 280 Mississippians with disabilities and Long COVID who are seeking to acquire CIE, resulting in at least 168 CIE or postsecondary educational placements.
  • Design and implement an innovative job retention intervention with 420 Mississippians with disabilities and Long COVID who are seeking to stay in the labor force, resulting in at least 357 CIE retention outcomes.
  • Utilize advanced technology devices and platforms to (a) ensure access to state-of-the-art workplace accommodations for project participants and (b) deliver transition and career navigation services in the modalities of participants’ choice.
  • Provide family engagement and social support services that promote CIE outcomes (e.g., personal care assistance, educational supports, independent living, advocacy, counseling and support groups, vocational rehabilitation and other employment services, healthcare and mental health, substance abuse treatment, transportation, housing, food security, online resources).
  • Provide training and technical assistance in CIE strategies to broad professional and public audiences invested in a fully inclusive Mississippi workforce.
  • Develop (a) an interactive project website and (b) a Long COVID and Employment Portal that will be hosted by the Job Accommodation Network (JAN) at West Virginia University (WVU).
